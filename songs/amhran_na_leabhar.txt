Go Cuan Bhéil Inse casadh mé
cois góilín aoibhinn Dairbhre
mar a seoltar flít na farraige
thar sáile i gcéin.

i bPort Magaoi do stadas seal,
fé thuairim intinn maitheasa
d'fhonn bheith sealad eatarthu
mar mháistir léinn.

Is gearr gur chuala an eachtra
ag cách mo léan
gur i mBord Eoghain Fhinn do chailleadh theas
an t-árthach tréan.

Do phreab mo chroí le hatuirse
'dtaobh loinge an taoisigh chalma
go mb'fhearrde an tír í 'sheasamh seal
do ráib an tséin.

Dá shiúlfainn Éire is Alba
an Fhrainc, an Spáinn is Sasana,
agus fós arís dá n-abarainn
gach aird faoin ré.

Ní bhfaighinnse an oiread leabhartha
b'fhearr eolas agus tairbhe
ná is mó bhí chum mo mhaitheasa
cé `táid ar strae.

Mo chreach, mo chumha ina n-easnamh súd
do fágadh mé,
is mór an cúrsa marbhna
agus cás liom é.

Mallacht Dé is na hEaglaise
ar an gcarraig ghránna mhallaithe,
do a bháigh an long gan anaithe
gan ghála, gan ghaoth.

Bhí mórán Éireann leabhartha,
nár áiríos díbh im labharthaibh,
leabhair na Laighneach beannaithe
ba bhreátha faoin spéir.

An feirmeoir álainn, gasta, deas,
do chuireadh a shíol go blasta ceart,
thug ruachnoic fraoigh is aitinn ghlais
go gealbhánta féir.

Scoirim as mo labharthaibh
cé chrádar mé,
is ná cuirfead aon ní ar fharraige
go brách lem ré;

Moladh le Rí na nAingeal ngeal,
mo shláinte arís do chasadh orm,
is an fhoireann úd ón anaithe
gan bá 'theacht saor.

Translation:

Go Cuan Bhéil Inse casadh mé
To the harbor of Valentia Island I happened to go

cois góilín aoibhinn Dairbhre
by the beuatiful inlet of Dairbhre

mar a seoltar flít na farraige
where the maritime goods are sailed 

thar sáile i gcéin.
overseas, far away


i bPort Magaoi do stadas seal,
In Portmagee I stopped a while

fé thuairim intinn maitheasa
for the sake of education

d'fhonn bheith sealad eatarthu
to be amongst them for a while

mar mháistir léinn.
as a schoolmaster


Is gearr gur chuala an eachtra
Soon the event was heard of

ag cách mo léan
by all, alas

gur i mBord Eonaín Fhinn do chailleadh theas
That the `Eonaín` was lost overboard

an t-árthach tréan.
the mighty vessel


Do phreab mo chroí le hatuirse
My heart throbbed with pain

'dtaobh loinge an taoisigh chalma
because the boat of that brave captain

go mb'fhearrde an tír í 'sheasamh seal
it was better for the country had it waited a while
 
do ráib an tséin.
to be under favorable auspices.


Dá shiúlfainn Éire is Alba
If only I could walk through Ireland and Scotland

an Fhrainc, an Spáinn is Sasana,
France, Spain and England

agus fós arís dá n-abarainn
and yet again if only I could tell

gach aird faoin ré.
every corner of the earth.

Ní bhfaighinnse an oiread leabhartha
I could not alas retrieve the multitude of books

b'fhearr eolas agus tairbhe
knowledge and usefulness

ná is mó bhí chum mo mhaitheasa
more than anything had shaped my belongings

cé `táid ar strae.
wgucg went astray

Mo chreach, mo chumha ina n-easnamh súd
Woe is me, my agony in that loss

do fágadh mé,
I was abandoned 

is mór an cúrsa marbhna
by theat cursed course

agus cás liom é.
over which I now grieve


Mallacht Dé is na hEaglaise
May the curse of God and the Church

ar an gcarraig ghránna mhallaithe,
be on those vile, deadly rocks

do a bháigh an long gan anaithe
for drowning the boat without a storm

gan ghála, gan ghaoth.
without a gale, without a wind.


Bhí mórán Éireann leabhartha,
There was a vast number of Irish books

nár áiríos díbh im labharthaibh,
which I didn't mention in my speeches

leabhair na Laighneach beannaithe
books of the blessed Leinstermen

ba bhreátha faoin spéir.
the finest on this earth.


An feirmeoir álainn, gasta, deas,
The delighful, clever, fine husbandman

do chuireadh a shíol go blasta ceart,
properly sowed his seed with relish

thug ruachnoic fraoigh is aitinn ghlais
he bestowed heather and green furze on rugged hills

go gealbhánta féir.
until the bright white hay


Scoirim as mo labharthaibh
I put a stop to my words

cé chrádar mé,
which afflicted me

is ná cuirfead aon ní ar fharraige
and I shall send nothing by sea

go brách lem ré;
ever again as long as I live.


Moladh le Rí na nAingeal ngeal,
Praise be to the bright king of the angels

mo shláinte arís do chasadh orm,
I regained my health

is an fhoireann úd ón anaithe
and from the storm yon crew

gan bá 'theacht saor.
without drowning safely returned.
