Irish

Do casadh cailín deas orm in uaigneas na dtrá,
Ar lúb na coille s'glaise uair a bheag roim lá:
Sé'n fhreagar’ ó a thug sí liom go ciúin agus go tláth:
Tá an saol ‘na gcoladh, is bogaimís an súisín bán.

(Chorus)
‘S má bhíonn tú liom bí liom, a stóirín mo chroí,
‘S má bhíonn tú liom bí liom os comhair a’ tí,
Má bhíonn tú liom, ‘s gur liom gach órlach ded’ chroí,
‘Sé mo mhíle chnoc nách liom Dé Domhnaigh tú mar mhnaoi.

Ó do bhíos seal im’ bhuachaill éadrom mhear ghroí,
‘S do bhíosa sheal agus d’imireoinn cárta le mnaoi 
Ó do bhíosa seal agus d'imireoinn cúig nó thrí-
Chun gur dhein a bhean so leongó liúngó dhom chroí.

(Chorus)

'S a Dhia na bhFeart, cad do chas ins a' dúthaigh seo mé?
Fé chlócaíbh dearga i bhfad óm' cháirdibh gael?
Ó do chuas isteach mar a raibh searc agus dian-ghrá mo chléibh,
'S chuir an t-seanbhean amach ag casadh an tsúgáin mé.

(Chorus)

A nice girl met me on the lonely beach
At the bend in the  green forest just before the day
Her answer to me was quiet and gentle:
“The world is in bed, so let us move the white blanket!”

(chorus)
And if you’re with me, be with me, love of my heart,
And if you’re with me, be with me in front of my house*,
If you’re with me, every inch of your heart will be with me,
It gives me a thousand regrets** that you won’t be with me Sunday as my wife

Oh awhile ago I was a vigorous, light-hearted boy
And awhile ago, and I would play cards with the women
Oh awhile ago, and I’d play five or three
Until this woman did make my heart go thump-thump***

Chorus

And Oh almighty God, what drove me to this land,
In red cloaks, far from my Irish friends?
Oh, I went inside, with tenderness and intense love in my heart.
And her old woman sent me out, twisting the rope

Chorus
